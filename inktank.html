Ink Tank

Overview

InkTank is a mobile app that allows users to browse and share tattoo designs and connect with local tattoo artists.

Purpose and Context

This mobile app was a project I completed during my UX Immersion course at CareerFoundry. I built Ink Tank from start to finish to illustrate the UX design process.

Role

UX Researcher, UX Designer, & UI Designer

Duration

The course project was designed to take 7 months if working at a rate of 15 hours per week, but it took me 8 months on and off to complete. My course was interrupted by a total of 9 months of medical leave.

Objective

Explore inspiration for tattoo designs according to preferences such as artist and style.

Hypothesis

User Interviews

I conducted interviews with 4 people who have tattoos. This research helped me to define their behaviors and attitudes, needs and goals, and frustrations. I mapped out their feelings and responses to key features of my app: tattoo inspiration, design collaboration and finding artists.

Personas

Based on the people I interviewed, I created 2 personas: Marcus who has more experience with tattoos and its industry and Natalie who may have no or little experience in anything tattoo related.

User Journeys

I created narratives to show how Marcus and Natalie would accomplish their goals using Ink Tank

User Flows

These diagrams show how Marcus and Natalie will interact with Ink Tank to complete a task. The boxes represent a page/screen from my app and the arrows indicate the direction of the flow.

Wireframes

I first sketched out my screens in pencil to create a low-fidelity prototype of the 3 core features of my app. It helped to see the flow of screens needed in order to complete Marcus and Natalie's tasks. Then, mid-fidelity prototypes were created in Balsamiq to visualize with more detail. Next, I used Sketch to make my high-fidelity prototype (in grayscale) which gave me a better sense of spacing and sizing of elements/assets. I used this prototype for user testing.

Responsive Frameworks
